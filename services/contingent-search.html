Our Contingent Search Process

Our efficient four-step approach ensures rapid identification of qualified candidates while maintaining the assessment rigor that distinguishes Carver Cross International.

1. Job Specification Review

We begin by thoroughly understanding your role requirements, organizational culture, and key success factors. This focused approach allows us to quickly grasp essential criteria and target appropriate candidate profiles for rapid sourcing.

2. Targeted Candidate Identification

Our research team activates our extensive talent database and network to identify qualified candidates who match your requirements. We focus on both active job seekers and professionals who have previously indicated openness to new opportunities.

3. Streamlined Assessment

Candidates undergo efficient yet thorough screening through structured interviews and skills verification. We evaluate technical competencies, leadership capability, and cultural fit to ensure only qualified candidates advance to your review process.

4. Candidate Presentation & Support

We present a select group of pre-screened candidates with detailed profiles highlighting their relevant experience and strengths. Our team facilitates the interview process, supports offer negotiations, and ensures a smooth transition for your selected candidate.

Contingent Search FAQs

What is contingent executive search?

Contingent executive search is a recruitment approach where payment is made only upon successful placement of a candidate. This flexible model provides access to our extensive talent network and rigorous candidate assessment process with a performance-based fee structure. It's ideal for organizations seeking quality executive talent without the commitment of a retained search arrangement.

How does contingent search differ from retained search?

Contingent search offers payment only upon successful placement, providing flexibility and reduced upfront commitment. It typically focuses on active job seekers and mid-level positions with faster timelines. Retained search involves an exclusive partnership with upfront fees, comprehensive market mapping of passive candidates, and is generally used for senior executive roles requiring in-depth assessment.

What positions are best suited for contingent search?

Contingent search is ideal for mid-level management positions, specialized technical roles, time-sensitive placements, non-confidential searches, and scenarios where multiple hiring options need to be evaluated. It provides excellent flexibility while still maintaining professional quality standards.

What is the typical timeline for a contingent search?

Contingent search typically delivers candidates within 2-4 weeks, with many initial profiles presented within the first 10 business days. The timeline depends on the role's specificity, location, and market conditions. Our process balances speed with thorough assessment to ensure quality matches despite the accelerated timeframe.

How do you maintain quality standards in contingent search?

Despite the contingent model's emphasis on speed, we maintain quality through structured interviews, technical assessments, reference verification, and cultural fit evaluation. Every candidate undergoes a standardized screening process to ensure they meet essential requirements before presentation to clients.

Can we work with multiple search firms on a contingent basis?

Yes, the contingent model allows for engagement with multiple search partners simultaneously. However, we find the most successful placements occur when clients provide detailed feedback and maintain open communication with their contingent partners, enabling more targeted candidate sourcing.
